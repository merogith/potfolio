A bit about me

Where the data work, the games, and the coaching all come from the same place.

I'm Meriç. I've always been curious about how things work — not just machines, but people, and the systems they build together. That curiosity is the through-line behind everything on this site.

It took me through environmental engineering at METU in Ankara, then to Berlin for a master's in Corporate Management. Two pretty different worlds, and I've spent my work since then living in the overlap between them.

The overlap is this: I read systems — financial, behavioural, technical. A business is a system you can read in numbers. Money is a system people feel anxious inside. A game is a system built to make someone feel something. Even my master's thesis was really about people: how they decide to trust a new technology. What ties it all together is wanting to understand both halves — the part you can measure, and the part you can't.

Day to day, I'm a data and business-intelligence analyst. I take data that's messy, incomplete, or qualitative, find what actually matters in it, and turn it into something a person can decide on — a clear report, a chart that earns its place, a recommendation with the uncertainty left honestly in. I care more about the decision than the dashboard. I'm not a data engineer or an ML engineer, and I won't pretend to be; my strength is the analysis and the judgment around it.

I also build. On the side I've shipped browser games — tested, with real engines behind them — which keeps me honest about how systems behave when you actually have to make them work, not just describe them. And I tend to be the person teams put between the technical side and everyone else: I can sit with an engineer and with a worried client in the same afternoon and help them understand each other. I listen more than I talk, and I'd rather get it right than get it loud.

That same instinct is why I also coach people on their relationship with money — not as a salesman, but as a guide who helps them feel calmer and more in control. (More on the coaching →)

Right now I'm looking for data, BI, and analytics work across the EU and remote — ideally somewhere that values careful thinking and isn't afraid of a new question. I'm in Berlin, authorized to work across the EU, and available to start now.

— Meriç
