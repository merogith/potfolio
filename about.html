A bit about me

The longer version: where I come from and what I work on.

I'm Meriç, a data and business-intelligence analyst based in Berlin. I've moved around a fair bit and changed direction more than once, but one thing has stayed constant: I want to understand how things work, and I'll usually take them apart to find out.

I was born in Ankara, grew up partly in İstanbul, then came back to Ankara for high school and university. I was the kind of kid who was glued to computers and games, always more interested in how the thing worked than in just playing it. That curiosity never really left.

I studied environmental engineering at METU in Ankara, one of Turkey's best universities and, just as importantly to me, one of its most independent and open-minded, even when the wider environment made that hard. The subject suited how my head works: it's about how living systems and built systems fit together, and it taught me to look at large, complex, interconnected systems in a structured way.

By the end of the degree I knew I wanted strategic, analytical work rather than field engineering. Alongside my studies I'd been teaching myself finance and investing through online courses and an internship, and I never stopped. I've been investing actively for about five years now; it's where a lot of my interest in markets and risk comes from, and it keeps my finance knowledge tied to real decisions rather than theory.

As things in Turkey grew harder, I moved to Germany: first to Hamburg for a master's in Corporate Management, then to Berlin, where I settled with my wife. Berlin is home now.

Day to day I work as a data and business-intelligence analyst. I take data that's messy, incomplete, or sometimes just qualitative, work out what matters in it, and turn that into something people can decide on. I care more about the decision than the dashboard. Outside of that I build my own projects, mostly browser games I design and test properly, and I coach people one-to-one on their relationship with money.

If there's a thread through all of it, it's how I like to work. I value collaboration and the human side of a problem as much as the technical part, I stay curious, I don't need much pushing to get going, and I'll back my own judgement when it counts. I'd rather understand something properly than look like I do.

I'm in Berlin, authorised to work across the EU, and open to data, BI, and analytics roles. If that sounds like a fit, I'd be glad to talk.

— Meriç
