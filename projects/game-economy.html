Free-to-Play Game Economy & Balance

Is the economy balanced, which items are broken, and who actually pays? The analysis a live-ops team runs on.

The question

Four live-ops questions decide the health of a free-to-play game: is the in-game economy balanced, are any items overpowered, how well does the game retain players, and who converts to paying? This project answers all four from the data.

The data

Synthetic gameplay, economy, and player data, generated with seeded scripts that add realistic noise — close enough to a real telemetry pipeline that the cleaning and modeling steps are meaningful.

Approach & method

  • Economy accounting: compared coins minted against coins removed by "sinks" to measure inflation.
  • Balance: proportion z-tests on item win rates to find statistically overpowered items.
  • Retention: built D1 / D7 / D30 cohort curves.
  • Monetization: logistic regression to predict paying players, scored by AUC.

What I found

  • The economy runs a mild inflation — sinks recover only ~63% of the coins minted.
  • Two premium items are statistically overpowered; the Phoenix Blade wins 61% of matches.
  • Retention drops sharply after day one: 47% → 18% → 4% at D1/D7/D30.
  • A logistic model predicts paying players with AUC 0.80.

Every finding maps to an action: add coin sinks, nerf two items, fix the day-1 drop-off, and target the players the model flags.

Why it matters

This is product / live-ops analytics end to end — hypothesis testing, cohort analysis, and predictive modeling tied to concrete game-design and monetization levers. It also pairs naturally with the games I build, so I understand both the telemetry and the system generating it.
