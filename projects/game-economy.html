The question

Three live-ops questions decide the health of a free-to-play game: is the in-game economy balanced (do faucets match sinks?), are any items overpowered and is the game pay-to-win, and what drives players to stay and pay? This project answers all three from the telemetry.

The data

Realistic synthetic telemetry: players, sessions, matches, a soft-currency ledger and purchases, generated by one seeded script that injects deliberate messiness (missing values, duplicate rows, mixed-case names, two timestamp formats, impossible negatives). Real F2P telemetry is proprietary and privacy-sensitive, so a reproducible synthetic generator was the practical path, and the cleaning steps are genuine.

Approach & method

  • Economy accounting: compared coins minted (faucets) against coins removed (sinks) to measure inflation, with a SQL SUM() OVER (PARTITION BY …) running wallet balance.
  • Item balance: proportion z-tests with 95% confidence intervals vs the fair 50% line to find statistically over/under-powered items.
  • Retention: built D1 / D7 / D30 cohort curves.
  • Monetisation: a funnel plus a logistic-regression payer model on first-3-day behaviour, scored by AUC and accuracy.

What I found

The economy is mildly inflationary: sinks recover only ~63% of every coin minted, so wallets steadily swell and the soft currency slowly loses value (net +6.8M coins over the window).

Two premium items are statistically overpowered: Phoenix Blade wins 61.3% and Aegis Shield 59.4%, with confidence intervals entirely above 50%. Because both are paid, the game reads as mildly pay-to-win. Rusty Dagger is the opposite, an underpowered starter trap at 41.8%.

Retention drops hard after the first day: D1 47%, D7 18%, D30 4%. About half of installs never return on day one, but the players who survive it are sticky, so the biggest lever is the first session.

Monetisation is small but predictable: only 5.8% of players ever pay (ARPPU $15.61), but a logistic model predicts payers from first-3-day behaviour alone with AUC 0.80 and ~74% accuracy.

Every finding maps to an action: add coin sinks, nerf two items, fix the day-1 drop-off, and target the players the model flags.

Recommendation

  • Re-balance the economy. Add or deepen a coin sink (cosmetics, repairs, a battle pass) so sinks recover ~90–100% of faucets, and trim late-game faucet creep. Target net daily flow near zero.
  • Nerf the two overpowered premium items toward 50% to kill the pay-to-win perception, and buff Rusty Dagger so the starter weapon isn't a trap.
  • Attack the D1 drop first with better onboarding, a guaranteed early win, and a D1 login reward. A few points of D1 cascade into every later metric.
  • Target likely payers early using the model: a well-timed, fair first offer to high-propensity players in their first days.

Limitations & next steps

The findings rest on synthetic data, so the methods matter more than the exact numbers. Next I'd design an A/B test for the sink and onboarding changes with explicit guardrail metrics, move from "ever pays" to predicted LTV with a churn-timing survival model, and track item balance over time with a win-rate control chart so new content can't silently break balance.
