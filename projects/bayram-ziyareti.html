Bayram-Ziyareti

A family-tree logic puzzle built on Turkish kinship words, where every move is real deduction.

Plays in your browser, no install

How to play

You're handed a family and a set of clue cards, and you drag faces into the right relationship slots by reasoning through the clues. It's built around Turkish's unusually precise kinship vocabulary: words like elti, bacanak, and dünür that don't map neatly to a single English word, so part of the puzzle is the language itself. There's a glossary if a term is new to you.

It comes in three modes: Soyağacı (matching the family tree), Bayram Sofrası (seating people by seniority), and Kız İsteme & Düğün (bride-side and groom-side scenarios).

Under the hood

The key idea is that a tile's kinship label isn't stored on it. Each relationship term is derived from the family graph (the spouse and parent edges plus genders) and recomputed as the tree takes shape. That derivation is the puzzle: you're not recalling a label, you're working out who someone must be.

Every clue is written twice: once as plain Turkish for the player, and once as a logic rule the computer can check. Before a puzzle ships, a solver confirms it has exactly one solution, so it's always fair, never a guess.

It's an installable PWA that works offline, the avatars are built from emoji and CSS rather than image files, and it's fully accessible, with ARIA throughout, reduced-motion support, and sound and haptics toggles.
