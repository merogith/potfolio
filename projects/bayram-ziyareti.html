Bayram-Ziyareti

A family-tree puzzle where Turkish kinship terms are computed from the graph, not stored — so every move is genuine deduction.

Play the game GitHub

What it is

A mobile-first puzzle game built around Turkish's unusually precise kinship vocabulary — terms like elti, bacanak, and dünür that have no single English equivalent. You drag faces into relationship slots using deductive clue cards, across three modes: Soyağacı (family-tree matching), Bayram Sofrası (seating by seniority), and Kız İsteme & Düğün (bride/groom-side scenarios).

The interesting part

Kinship terms are derived from the family graph (spouse/parent edges plus genders), not stored on each tile — that derivation is the puzzle. Clues are authored as both human Turkish text and machine-checkable predicates, so an authoring-time solver can guarantee every puzzle has a unique solution (provably fair). It's an installable PWA with offline support, avatars composed from emoji + CSS (no image assets), and full accessibility (ARIA, reduced-motion, sound/haptics toggles).
