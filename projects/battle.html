Battle

My most complete project: a full turn-based RPG with online PvP, built from scratch and refined across 1,699+ commits.

What it is

A Pokémon-style turn-based battler with a full story mode, persistent saves, party management, and — the hard part — online player-vs-player over a Supabase backend. It runs on a custom vanilla-JavaScript game engine, with no game framework underneath.

How it plays

  • Turn-based combat with move animations, status effects, and field hazards.
  • A story mode with saved progress, plus catch / PC / Underground / Safari systems and boss arcs.
  • HP and status fully restore between battles, while consumables matter within an encounter — so each fight is a self-contained puzzle.
  • Battle the AI through the campaign, or take on another person in online PvP.

The engineering (the interesting part)

  • A custom engine in vanilla JS — turn loop, move resolution, status/hazard system — with no framework.
  • A Supabase / PostgreSQL backend (with migrations) powering online PvP and persistence.
  • An automated test suite with deterministic replay: the same seed reproduces a run exactly, so bugs are reproducible and regressions get caught.
  • Performance profiling of the turn loop, move parsing, and memory.
  • A structured debug / QA system with a centralized issue ledger and spec-drift checks against the design docs.
  • A PWA manifest for installable, app-like behavior.

1,699+ commits of solo, iterative engineering — design docs, migrations, tests, and profiling. This is a maintained system, not a weekend prototype.

Why it matters

Battle shows I can design, build, and maintain a large, stateful, networked system on my own — frontend, backend, and a genuine testing and QA discipline. It's the same rigor I bring to data work, applied to software: reproducibility, measurement, and a clear contract between the parts.
