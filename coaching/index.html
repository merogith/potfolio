Is this for you?

This is for you if…

Money makes you anxious

You avoid checking your accounts, or you check them and feel worse. You'd like that to ease up.

You're just starting out

First real salary, first time investing, first time it's all on you. You want to start on the right foot.

You're new to Germany

A new country and a new financial system, often in a language that isn't your first. We can work in Turkish or English.

You're busy

You roughly know what you should be doing. You just want someone to keep you on track and answer questions as they come up.

My approach

How I think about money coaching

This is closer to learning a skill than getting advice. The aim isn't a hot tip you'll forget by next week. It's that you understand your own money well enough to make your own decisions.

It starts with you, not a spreadsheet

We start with how you actually feel and behave around money, because that's usually where budgets fall apart.

Education, in plain language

Budgeting, saving, and how investing actually works, explained in a way that stays with you.

You're not doing it alone

This isn't a one-off lecture. We meet regularly, and your plan changes as your life does.

I don't believe in quick fixes or hot tips. They rarely last, and they keep you dependent on whoever handed them to you. My job is the opposite: to help you understand your own money and habits well enough that, in time, you don't need me. I'll be there while that takes hold.

How it works

From the first call onward

A free intro call

Half an hour on where you are and what you'd like to change. No pitch. If I'm not the right person for you, I'll say so and point you somewhere better.

A plan that fits your situation

Built around your income, your worries, and your goals, not a template. We decide together what "better" actually means for you.

A weekly rhythm

Short weekly check-ins, plus a longer session of about 60 minutes when you need it. More support at the start, less as things settle.

Support between sessions

A monthly newsletter, the occasional "have you thought about this?", and someone to check in with while the new habit forms.

What changes

What you'll come away with

  • A budget you'll actually use, not a spreadsheet you abandon in week two.
  • A clear picture of where your money goes each month.
  • Enough understanding to read a payslip, a fund, or a German tax letter without panic.
  • A short written plan you understand and can follow on your own.

Questions

The things people ask first

What does it cost?

Sessions are €[XX] each, and we'll confirm the right setup for you on the intro call. Most people start with a weekly rhythm and ease off as things settle. The intro call is free with no obligation, and if money is genuinely tight, tell me and we'll find something that works.

Which languages do you work in?

English or Turkish, whichever you think more clearly in. Money is emotional, and it helps to talk about it in your own language.

Is this financial advice?

No. It's coaching and education. I won't tell you which fund to buy or manage your investments. I help you understand your options and your own habits so you can decide well. If you need regulated advice, I'll say so and point you to a licensed adviser.

Where do sessions happen, and how often?

Online, wherever you are. Usually weekly to start, then less often as things settle.

Do I need to be "good with money" already?

Not at all. Most people come precisely because they're not, and that's fine. We start where you are, no judgment.
